Theoretical Chemistry

Theoretical Chemistry broadly deals with modeling physical and chemical phenomena from first principles of interacting systems. In our group we deal with the most fundamental microscopic regime of interacting electrons and nuclei. We build and use models describing these interactions with quantum mechanics to describe bonding, reactivity and spectroscopy. Our group is dynamic and flexible and we are forever expanding our horizons. Please feel free to contact us to know about our most recent endeavors. Some of our fields of interest are described below..

Quantum Chemistry

Models for Electron Correlation


Coupled Cluster, a powerful cornerstone of computational quantum chemistry, stands at the heart of our research endeavors. With its ability to provide highly accurate predictions for molecular properties and interactions, we harness the potential of Coupled Cluster to unravel the intricacies of complex systems. Through meticulous calculations that consider electron correlation effects, we unlock insights into molecular structures, reaction mechanisms, and energetic landscapes. Our research lab is committed to pushing the boundaries of Coupled Cluster methodology, advancing our understanding of chemical phenomena, and contributing to innovations across various scientific disciplines.
